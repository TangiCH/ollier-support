About Ollier Disease

Ollier disease is a rare sporadic nonhereditary skeletal disorder in which typically benign cartilaginous tumors (enchondromas) develop near the growth plate cartilage. Key signs include asymmetry and shortening of the limb. These symptoms are typically first visible during early childhood. The prevalence is estimated at around 1 in 100,000.

Our Community

The journey with a rare diagnosis can be difficult, but you don't have to walk it alone. Our group brings together people with Ollier disease from different places to:

  • Share personal stories and daily challenges.
  • Exchange tips for managing symptoms and treatments.
  • Find emotional support in a safe and understanding environment.
